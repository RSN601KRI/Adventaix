03. Africa Forest tour

The Congo Basin rainforest in Africa is the most extensive and biodiverse tropical forest in the world, covering over 1.5 million square kilometers. It boasts a rich variety of wildlife, including gorillas and elephants, and plays a crucial role in regulating the Earth's climate by absorbing carbon dioxide. However, it faces threats from deforestation and habitat loss.

07. Japan Forest tour

The Aokigahara Forest, often referred to as the "Sea of Trees," is one of Japan's most famous forests, located at the base of Mount Fuji. Its eerie reputation stems from being a site associated with suicides. Despite its haunting history, Aokigahara is also known for its lush vegetation, unique lava formations, and tranquil hiking trails, attracting nature enthusiasts and tourists alike.

Epping Forest, located on the northeastern outskirts of London, is one of the city's most popular forests.
